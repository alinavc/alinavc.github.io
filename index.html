The chameleon is the lizard best known for camouflaging and blending into its surroundings, utilizing its color palette unique to its species. It has a long curly tail, which is used to secure itself on a branch or a leaf. Chameleons have eyes that allow full 360-degree movement and each eye can be looking in a different direction at the same time. Lastly, the tongue of the chameleon is quite unique. Since chameleons tend to move slowly, they rely on their quick and sticky tongues to catch their dinner. If you would like to learn more about chameleons, you can visit the San Diego Zoo Wildlife Alliance.

5 Chameleon Facts

  1. Chameleons change color to regulate body temperature
  2. There are 200+ different chameleon species
  3. Chameleons have good eyesight
  4. Chameleons can take 4-24 months to hatch
  5. The lifespan of a chameleon can range from 2-10 years
